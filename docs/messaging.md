# Messaging & Positioning

The single source of truth for how Campbooks describes itself. Keep user-facing copy
across the **app**, the **marketing website**, and **in-repo docs** consistent with this.

> **Consistency, not uniformity.** Don't robotically repeat the same line everywhere — vary
> the phrasing to fit the context. Just never *contradict* what's below: same product, same
> audience, same promise, wherever we say it.

## What Campbooks is (the one-liner)

**The email client that declutters your work** — an AI-native inbox that sorts itself,
reimagined so it feels nothing like the email you're used to.

It's an *email client*, not a "document management platform" and not just a "document
manager." It happens to also turn your attachments into tracked documents — but the category
we lead with is email/inbox.

## Who it's for

**Young professionals and small-business owners** who live in business email and paperwork
and find legacy email (Gmail, Outlook) tedious, static, and dated. Both audiences share the
same job-to-be-done; we don't pick one over the other.

## The promise

**Declutter.** Cut the noise, surface what actually needs you, file the rest — so your
attention goes to decisions, not drudgery.

## What makes it different (the heart)

It is **not a skin on email**. The whole experience is reimagined — how you triage, read,
and act feels modern, fast, and genuinely satisfying. The auto-sorting is the hook; *the way
it does everything differently* is the reason people stay. If a piece of copy only says "it
sorts your inbox," it's underselling.

## USP pillars

Lead with whichever fits the context — don't list all six every time.

1. **It sorts itself** — Scout reads, classifies, and files your email and attachments; you
   get one short list of what actually needs you.
2. **A different kind of inbox** — fast, satisfying triage built for how you work now, not
   for 2012. The UX is the product.
3. **Email + paperwork, together** — invoices, receipts, and contracts become searchable,
   tracked documents with a clear review-and-approval flow.
4. **Scout, your assistant** — briefs you, answers questions about your inbox and documents
   in plain language, and drafts replies you can approve.
5. **Yours to run** — **free to self-host for your own business or personal use**, on your own
   server; source-available, private and EU-hosted, no lock-in. The license only draws the line at
   reselling it or running it as a service for others.
6. **Your data, your rules** — you stay in control of where your data lives and which AI, if any,
   touches it: one switch pauses all AI, you can bring your own AI keys or point at your own
   model, or use the EU-default managed AI — and the app shows you which provider and region
   handles each task. Export or delete your data whenever you want.

## Voice & tone

Warm, human, confident — like a competent friend, not an enterprise vendor. Plain language;
no jargon, no fintech sterility. The **interface feels alive**; the **result feels calm**.
Use "calm" for the *outcome* (relief, headspace), never as the product's defining adjective.

**What Scout actually does (be honest):** Scout *reads, sorts, surfaces, and drafts* — it does **not** act on your behalf in the background, send anything without approval, or resolve work for you. Never write copy implying it "handled," "cleared," or "took care of" things on its own, and never make a large pile sound effortless ("249 things… Scout handled the rest" is both untrue and tone-deaf). Acknowledge real volume honestly and point to the triage/skim flow.

## Vocabulary

**Use:** email client · inbox · declutter · sorts itself · Scout · paperwork / documents ·
reimagined · surface what needs you · source-available · fair-code · free to self-host · yours to run ·
you control your data · which AI sees your data · pause AI · bring your own keys / your own model ·
EU-default managed AI · export or delete anytime.

**Avoid:** "document management platform" or "document manager" as the *primary* label (it
undersells the email half) · "Sign in to manage your documents" and similar doc-only framings
· "calm" as the headline adjective for the product itself · "open source" / "MIT" for Campbooks
itself (the license is the Sustainable Use License, a fair-code source-available license) ·
**absolute privacy claims** like "your data never leaves the EU", "fully private", or "we can't
see your data" — managed document analysis uses a US provider and hosted data isn't end-to-end
encrypted yet, so promise the *controls* (pause AI, bring your own keys, EU-default, export/delete),
not absolutes.

## Approved lines (reuse freely)

- **Hero / primary:** "The inbox that sorts itself."
- **Secondary tagline:** "Your paperwork, sorted."
- **Lede:** "Campbooks reads your email and attachments, files the invoices, receipts, and
  contracts on its own, and hands you one short list of what actually needs you."
- **Self-host / "Yours to run":** "Read, modify, and self-host Campbooks for free, for your own
  business or personal use." When there's room, add the one limit: "The license only restricts
  reselling it or running it as a commercial service for others." Lead with the *permission*
  (you can run it yourself, free) — never just "view the source," which undersells the right.
- **Data & privacy:** "You decide which AI sees your email — pause it entirely, bring your own
  keys, or use our EU-default managed AI — and you can export or delete your data anytime."
